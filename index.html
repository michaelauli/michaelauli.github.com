Michael Auli

My research focuses on applying deep learning to natural language processing, and specifically machine translation. Previously, I worked on improving supervised parsing using Combinatory Categorial Grammar (CCG) which led to the best reported results on CCGbank.

I am currently a postdoc researcher at Microsoft Research in Redmond, WA. Previously, I received my Ph.D. from the School of Informatics at the University of Edinburgh supervised by Philipp Koehn and Adam Lopez. I also had the good fortune to collaborate with Prachya Boonkwan, David Chiang, Stephen Clark, Michel Galley, Jianfeng Gao, Hieu Hoang, Yuening Hu, Kevin Knight, Tom Kwiatkowski, Arul Menezes, Adam Pauls, Chris Quirk, Mark Steedman, Charles Sutton, Luke Zettlemoyer, and Geoffrey Zweig.
